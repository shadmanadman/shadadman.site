## oct-12-2014-AlfaTechnology-First-Sprint

This marks the beginning of an amazing era. This is my first official company based job. I have worked as a freelancer and will always return to it when needed. However, working with a professional team brings a new, exciting dimension to my job as a developer.

This small organization had a fabulous idea. They developed an SMS based hardware that attaches to a car, allowing users to create an advanced remote control with no distance limitations. The car owner can lock/unlock the car doors, turn the car on/off, and the hardware can send alarms directly to the phone in case of a collision or unexpected door opening.

They needed an Android Developer to create an app that detects this SMS pattern. Users could then use the app instead of sending raw SMS messages. They had a nice design for the home page, which looked like a real car remote control.

The first two weeks were spent familiarizing myself with the hardware, thinking of our Android project arch, the SMS patterns, and doing R&D on reading SMS on Android devices, as well as making good friends among my team, which included a designer, my CTO, and our hardware team.

I had also worked with our CEO, Ata Amini, on a small freelance project, and that was the beginning of our working relationship.
